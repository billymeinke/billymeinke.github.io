Hi!

My name is Billy. I'm a technologist that works on open learning environments.

If you're reading this page, you probably want to know more about me.

I work for the Distance Course Design and Consulting (DCDC) Group in the College of Education at the University of Hawaii at Manoa. I've had a hand in many educational technology initiatives over the last several years, focusing on projects that support open, distributed learning. These days I plan and design higher education courses and programs, build bicycles, surf some, and tinker with Web technology that supports learning and creativity.

I facilitate workshops and speak at conferences, and occasionally take on creative projects. If you're interested in getting in touch with me about an opportunity, head over to the contact page.

Blogroll

Every good blog should have a blogroll. Here's mine.

  • Stephen Downes / OLDaily - Stephen sends an almost-daily email with links and commentary about a batch of mostly interesting edtech articles and happenings.
  • Paul Stacey / Edtech Frontier - Paul's personal blog that chronicals his involvement in open education, open business models, and much more.
  • David Wiley / opencontent - David created open copyright licenses before they were a thing, and has shared many open education ponderings since.
  • Martin Weller / The Ed Techie - Martin's personal blog where he shares his views on higher education in general, and his broad expertise in open education.
  • OER Research Hub - Blog of the OER Hub at The Open University (UK), where a network of open education researchers share their latest work.
  • Audrey Watters / Hack Education - One of the most honest not-gonna-pull-punches blogs about edtech that you'll find.
