Hi! I'm Billy.
I work on infrastructure.

Most of my work takes place at the intersection of education, science, and free culture. I've had a hand in projects that are making access to education ubiquitous and free. I've worked alongside scientists who manipulate and compute data right in the browser. I've collaborated with technologists who see a very bright future for those who want to build things together. I want to build more things.


The real deal.

I'm a California transplant living in Honolulu, Hawaii, on the island of Oahu. I studied social science at the University of California Santa Barbara and educational technology at the University of Hawaii at Manoa. I interned and worked at Creative Commons beginning in 2012, and focused my effort helping others explore what it means for "science to be open", and building community among stakeholders of the world's largest OER project.



Work

Here are click-through examples of work I've done over the last several years.


Design + Dev

I led the development of the CC Toolkits project, a combination of coordinated content sprints and design of a friendly, remixable template for curating support resources about Creative Commons. Site made with Jekyll, Bootstrap, and Github Pages.


Writing

I wrote vignette pieces for CC's Team Open, showing the impact of openness in scientific research and data through stories of people who leveraged openness to do incredible things.




OER Fellowship

In 2014 I was a visiting fellow of the OER Research Hub, collaborating on research and open course development. I wrote several blog posts documenting my experience and sharing the results of my survey about OER adoption in the U.S. DOL TAACCCT program.
